## 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1. **"Business"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Information (equivalent to Data Controller).

1.2. **"Service Provider"** means the entity that processes Personal Information on behalf of the Business (equivalent to Data Processor).

1.3. **"Personal Information"** means information that identifies, relates to, describes, or could reasonably be linked to a particular consumer or household.

1.4. **"Consumer"** means a natural person who is a California resident or resident of applicable state.

1.5. **"CCPA"** means California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by CPRA.

1.6. **"State Privacy Laws"** means applicable state privacy laws including CCPA/CPRA, VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, etc.

## 2. US-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1. CCPA/CPRA Compliance
- **Service Provider Restrictions:** Service Provider shall not:
  - Sell Personal Information
  - Share Personal Information for cross-context behavioral advertising
  - Retain, use, or disclose Personal Information except as necessary to perform Services
  - Combine Personal Information with other data except as permitted

### 2.2. Consumer Rights
Service Provider shall assist Business in responding to Consumer requests:
- Right to know (access)
- Right to delete
- Right to correct
- Right to opt-out of sale/sharing
- Right to limit use of sensitive personal information
- Right to non-discrimination

### 2.3. Sensitive Personal Information
If processing Sensitive Personal Information:
- Obtain explicit consent for processing
- Provide opt-out mechanism
- Limit use to specified purposes
- Implement additional security measures

## 3. STATE-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS

### 3.1. California (CCPA/CPRA)
- **Do Not Sell/Share:** Service Provider shall not sell or share Personal Information
- **Opt-Out:** Service Provider shall honor opt-out requests
- **Non-Discrimination:** Service Provider shall not discriminate against Consumers

### 3.2. Virginia (VCDPA)
- **Controller-Processor Relationship:** Service Provider acts as Processor
- **Data Subject Rights:** Assist with Consumer rights requests
- **Data Protection Assessments:** Assist with assessments when required

### 3.3. Colorado (CPA)
- **Universal Opt-Out:** Service Provider shall honor universal opt-out signals
- **Data Minimization:** Process only necessary Personal Information
- **Purpose Limitation:** Use Personal Information only for specified purposes

### 3.4. Connecticut (CTDPA)
- **Consumer Rights:** Assist with Consumer rights requests
- **Data Minimization:** Process only necessary Personal Information
- **Security Measures:** Implement reasonable security measures

## 5. FINANCIAL DATA (GLBA)

5.1. If processing financial information:
- Comply with Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
- Implement financial privacy safeguards
- Provide privacy notices
- Protect against unauthorized access

## 6. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

6.1. Service Provider shall:
- Notify Business without undue delay (within 24 hours) of any breach
- Provide all information necessary for Business to comply with state breach notification laws
- Assist Business in notifying affected Consumers if required

6.2. **State Notification Requirements:**
- **California:** Notify within 72 hours if > 500 residents affected
- **Other States:** As per applicable state law (typically 30-60 days)

---

## 7. AUDIT RIGHTS

7.1. Business may:
- Request audit of Service Provider's compliance
- Request copies of security certifications (SOC2, ISO 27001)
- Conduct on-site audits with 30 days' notice

---

## 8. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

8.1. **Retention:** Service Provider shall retain Personal Information only:
- For duration specified in Agreement
- As required by applicable law
- As necessary for Service Provider's legitimate business purposes

8.2. **Deletion:** Upon termination or request:
- Delete or return all Personal Information within 30 days
- Delete all copies, including backups, within 90 days
- Provide written confirmation of deletion
