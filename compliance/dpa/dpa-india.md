## 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1. **"Data Fiduciary"** means the entity that determines the purpose and means of processing Personal Data (equivalent to Data Controller under GDPR).

1.2. **"Data Processor"** means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary.

1.3. **"Personal Data"** means any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data.

1.4. **"DPDPA"** means the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023.

1.5. **"Data Principal"** means the individual to whom Personal Data relates (equivalent to Data Subject under GDPR).

---

## 2. INDIA-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1. Data Localization
- **Financial Data:** As per RBI guidelines, financial data must be stored in India
- **Processing:** Processing may occur in India or approved jurisdictions
- **Backup:** Backups may be stored outside India with encryption

### 2.2. Consent Management
- **Explicit Consent:** Explicit consent required for processing Personal Data
- **Consent Withdrawal:** Data Principal may withdraw consent at any time
- **Consent Records:** Maintain records of consent for audit purposes

### 2.3. Data Principal Rights
Processor shall assist Fiduciary in responding to Data Principal requests:
- Right to access Personal Data
- Right to correction and erasure
- Right to grievance redressal
- Right to nominate representative

### 2.4. Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF)
If Fiduciary is classified as SDF, additional requirements apply:
- Data Protection Officer (DPO) appointment
- Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
- Periodic audits
- Additional security measures

---

## 3. DATA LOCALIZATION

3.1. **Primary Storage:** Personal Data shall be stored in India as required by:
- Reserve Bank of India (RBI) guidelines
- Data localization requirements
- Sector-specific regulations

3.2. **Processing:** Processing may occur in:
- India (primary)
- Approved jurisdictions (with appropriate safeguards)

3.3. **Cross-Border Transfer:** Any cross-border transfer shall:
- Comply with DPDPA requirements
- Use appropriate safeguards (encryption, contractual clauses)
- Obtain necessary approvals

---

## 4. RBI COMPLIANCE

4.1. **Financial Data:** For financial services data:
- Data must be stored in India
- Processing must comply with RBI guidelines
- Audit trail must be maintained

4.2. **Payment Data:** Payment-related data:
- Stored in India
- Encrypted at rest and in transit
- Access logged and audited

---

## 5. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL

5.1. Processor shall:
- Assist Fiduciary in establishing grievance redressal mechanism
- Respond to Data Principal grievances within 30 days
- Maintain records of grievances and resolutions

---

## 6. AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

6.1. **Regulatory Audits:** Processor shall:
- Cooperate with regulatory audits (RBI, etc.)
- Provide necessary documentation
- Maintain audit logs for 7 years

6.2. **Compliance Certifications:** Processor maintains:
- ISO 27001 certification
- SOC2 Type II (in progress)
- Regular security assessments

---

## 7. DATA RETENTION

7.1. **Retention Period:** As per:
- DPDPA requirements
- RBI guidelines (7 years for financial data)
- Sector-specific regulations

7.2. **Deletion:** Upon termination or request:
- Delete Personal Data within 30 days
- Provide deletion confirmation
- Maintain deletion logs
