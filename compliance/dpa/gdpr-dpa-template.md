## 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1. **"Data Controller"** means the entity which determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data.

1.2. **"Data Processor"** means the entity which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Controller.

1.3. **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

1.4. **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, and deletion.

1.5. **"Data Subject"** means the natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

1.6. **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data.

1.7. **"GDPR"** means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (General Data Protection Regulation).

---

## 2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE

2.1. This DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services.

2.2. **Processing Details:**
   - **Subject Matter:** Financial data, transaction data, user account data
   - **Duration:** For the term of the Agreement
   - **Nature:** Storage, analysis, reporting, financial modeling
   - **Purpose:** Provision of financial planning and analysis services
   - **Categories of Data Subjects:** Controller's employees, customers, partners
   - **Types of Personal Data:** Names, email addresses, financial information, transaction data

---

## 3. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1. Processing Instructions
Processor shall:
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller
- Not process Personal Data for any purpose other than as specified in this DPA
- Immediately inform Controller if Processor believes an instruction violates GDPR

### 3.2. Confidentiality
Processor shall:
- Ensure persons authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations
- Maintain confidentiality of Personal Data
- Not disclose Personal Data to third parties except as permitted by this DPA

### 3.3. Security Measures
Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure security of Personal Data, including:
- Encryption of data at rest (AES-256)
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security assessments
- Incident response procedures
- Data backup and recovery procedures

### 3.4. Sub-processors
Processor may engage Sub-processors with Controller's prior written consent. Processor shall:
- Maintain list of Sub-processors (available upon request)
- Notify Controller of any intended changes to Sub-processors
- Ensure Sub-processors are bound by same data protection obligations
- Remain fully liable for Sub-processor's performance

**Current Sub-processors:**
- **AWS (Amazon Web Services):** Cloud infrastructure (US, EU, India regions)
- **PostgreSQL:** Database services (encrypted)
- **Stripe:** Payment processing (if applicable)

### 3.5. Data Subject Rights
Processor shall assist Controller in responding to Data Subject requests, including:
- Right of access (Article 15)
- Right to rectification (Article 16)
- Right to erasure (Article 17)
- Right to restriction of processing (Article 18)
- Right to data portability (Article 20)
- Right to object (Article 21)

### 3.6. Data Breach Notification
Processor shall:
- Notify Controller without undue delay (within 24 hours) of any Personal Data breach
- Provide all information necessary for Controller to comply with GDPR Article 33
- Assist Controller in notifying supervisory authority and Data Subjects if required

### 3.7. Data Protection Impact Assessment
Processor shall assist Controller in conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) when required under GDPR Article 35.

### 3.8. Records of Processing
Processor shall maintain records of processing activities as required by GDPR Article 30(2).

## 4. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

4.1. Controller warrants that:
- It has legal basis for Processing Personal Data
- It has obtained necessary consents from Data Subjects
- Processing instructions comply with applicable data protection laws
- Personal Data is accurate and up-to-date

4.2. Controller shall:
- Provide accurate and complete instructions to Processor
- Respond promptly to Processor's requests for assistance
- Notify Processor of any changes affecting Processing

---

## 5. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

5.1. **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):** Where Personal Data is transferred outside the EEA/UK, Processor shall:
- Use EU Commission-approved Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2: Controller to Processor)
- Implement supplementary measures as required
- Comply with UK Addendum for UK transfers

5.2. **Data Residency:** Controller may specify data residency requirements:
- **EU/UK:** Data stored in EU/UK regions only
- **US:** Data stored in US regions only
- **India:** Data stored in India regions only
- **Global:** Data may be stored in any region

5.3. **Transfer Impact Assessment:** Processor shall provide Transfer Impact Assessment upon request.

---

## 6. AUDIT RIGHTS

6.1. Controller may:
- Request audit of Processor's compliance with this DPA
- Request copies of Processor's security certifications (SOC2, ISO 27001)
- Conduct on-site audits with 30 days' notice (limited to business hours)

6.2. Processor shall:
- Provide reasonable assistance for audits
- Maintain audit logs and documentation
- Allow access to relevant facilities and records

---

## 7. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

7.1. **Retention Period:** Processor shall retain Personal Data only for:
- Duration specified in the Agreement
- Period required by applicable law
- Period necessary for Processor's legitimate business purposes

7.2. **Deletion:** Upon termination of the Agreement or Controller's request, Processor shall:
- Delete or return all Personal Data within 30 days
- Delete all copies, including backups, within 90 days
- Provide written confirmation of deletion

7.3. **Retention Exceptions:** Processor may retain Personal Data if required by law, provided Processor:
- Informs Controller of retention requirement
- Limits Processing to legal requirement
- Protects Personal Data with appropriate security measures

---

## 8. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

8.1. Each party shall be liable for its own breaches of this DPA.

8.2. Processor's liability for data protection breaches is limited as set forth in the Agreement, except where:
- Breach results from Processor's gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Breach results in violation of Data Subject rights

8.3. Controller shall indemnify Processor against claims arising from:
- Controller's breach of this DPA
- Controller's unlawful Processing instructions
- Controller's failure to obtain necessary consents

---

## 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1. This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Agreement.

9.2. Upon termination:
- Processor shall delete or return Personal Data as specified in Section 7
- Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination

10.2. **Disputes:** Any disputes shall be resolved in accordance with the Agreement's dispute resolution provisions.

10.3. **Severability:** If any provision is invalid, remaining provisions shall remain in effect.

10.4. **Amendments:** This DPA may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.
